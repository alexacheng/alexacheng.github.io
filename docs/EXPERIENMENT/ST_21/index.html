    Soft Touch - A collaborative musical instrument concept

    This small conceptual project was completed during DigitalFutures workshop – Soft Touch, instructed by professor Sofia Guridi and Emmi Pouta from Aalto University in Finland. The workshop aimed at combining electronic components with flexible textile materials to obtain some further product solutions or concepts.

    North pole is shifting. According to researchers,  due to the severe climate change, the pole has been moving steadily eastward by about 75 degrees, heading toward the Prime Meridian that runs through Greenwich, England.

    This shift has been on the order of 10 centimeters a year, so it’s probably not enough to warrant a recalculation of the planet’s geographic pole–although later generations may have to consider it if things keep changing. Which has also left the animals that lives there… with serious problems.

    By using capacitive sensor (metallic foil + Arduino) , I made the effect of “touching on surface to control the LED lighting” prototype on paper cup at first. Then use 3d printing to test on product case.
